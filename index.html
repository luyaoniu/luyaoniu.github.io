About Me

I am an Assistant Teaching Professor in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Washington. I received my Ph.D. degree in 2022 under the supervision of Prof. Andrew Clark from the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Worcester Polytechnic Institute. I received an MS in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Worcester Polytechnic Institute. My research focus is to develop scalable algorithms and methodologies to establish trustworthy autonomy in adversarial environments by encompassing (1) safe AI, (2) robust control and verification, and (3) resilient cyber systems. These solutions use an interplay of methodologies from machine learning, optimization and control, game theory, and formal methods. Potential application domains of the proposed solutions include power systems, smart transportation, and robotics.
